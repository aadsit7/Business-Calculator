Methodology, basis, sources & risks

Derivation (workload from the book). Distinct apps = apps/customer × (overlap + customers × (1 − overlap)) — the shared core counted once; each customer adds only unique line-of-business apps. Packaging requests/mo = distinct apps × repackage rate. Patch ops = distinct apps × per-app minutes. App tickets = seats × per-100-seat rate. Staffing follows workload, not user population (MetricNet).

Motions. ① Cost lines = hours × loaded rate; platform column adds license; net = gross labor reduction − license. ② Service revenue = attached seats × price; cost to serve = attached seats × with-AW cost/seat — the partner sets scope and price (guide). ③ Readiness labor uses the same per-app hours × environments engine on project scope; schedule value = weeks × weekly project cost, applicable only when readiness is critical-path. Value-driver attribution is Shapley — parts sum exactly to motion-① gross.

  1. Recast MSP & SI Partner Guide (Jul 2026). Three commercial motions; proof metrics (cost to serve, labor hours, request-to-access, install/launch success, SLA, renewal); managed packages still require partner testing, approval and rollout policy; Setup Store full breadth requires Release & Patch Management (base license limited); confirm SKU and licensing before commitments.
  2. App portfolios. Okta Businesses at Work 2025: average 101 apps/org; large (≥2,000 employees) ~211; smallest ~22.
  3. Packaging effort. Camwood: packaging = 30–40% of IT-admin time at 60–70% first-pass success. Recast: 4+ hrs/app; small orgs repackage 5–10/mo; complex apps 8–40 hrs (community benchmarks). Estates carry 2–4 package formats.
  4. Tickets. Software & applications = over a third of desk volume (2026 benchmark); ~0.5–1+ tickets/user/mo (MetricNet); default 6 app tickets/100 seats is conservative. Catalog self-service deflects 50–70% of software requests.
  5. Labor cost. BLS median pay for systems administrators / support specialists plus the private-industry benefit share underpins a US loaded rate of roughly $70–95/hr; offshore rate guides: India $20–45, blended $30–60 — selector mid-points $95/$65/$40.
  6. Per-package cost cross-check. A 2025 Windows packaging cost study (Juriba) reports ≈$646 average per package — ≈9 hrs at a $70 loaded rate — consistent with the 4.33-hr standard × 2.5 environments basis here.
  7. Ticket volume cross-check. MetricNet ≈1.1 agent-assisted tickets/user/mo × Fixify 2026 (software & applications = 38.2% of volume) ⇒ ≈0.42 app tickets/user/mo; the 0.20 default (20 per 100 seats) is deliberately conservative. MetricNet cites ≈15-min handling for business-app tickets and calls a 25% annual ticket reduction excellent progress.
  8. MSP economics. Recurring-services gross margin 50–60%; labor 90%+ of service COGS (Service Leadership / ConnectWise).
  • Margin recovery must be measured against a baseline (guide) — the derived labor lines are that baseline; validate in a proof-of-value.
  • Each Application Workspace zone (per customer) requires licensing — confirm the multi-tenant commercial structure with Recast before pricing the SKU.
  • Motions are additive only where scope is contractually distinct; treat motion ② as incremental revenue, not a re-count of motion ① savings.
  • Managed-service inclusions depend on licensed capabilities; Setup Store breadth varies by edition — confirm SKU, edition and partner-tier license before commitments.
  • Project acceleration applies only where application readiness is on the critical path; it cannot remove unrelated security, infrastructure, licensing or business delays (guide).
  • Managed packages still require partner testing, approval and rollout — reflected in non-zero AW per-app hours.
  • Excludes one-time onboarding/migration/enablement; dollar figures scale with the labor rate, hours and FTE do not; planning model, not a measured baseline.
